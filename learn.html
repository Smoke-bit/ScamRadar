Scam Prevention Checklist

Follow these simple habits to significantly reduce your chances of becoming an online scam victim.

✔

Never share your OTP or banking PIN with anyone.

✔

Always verify website URLs before entering personal information.

✔

Double-check UPI IDs before making payments.

✔

Ignore messages creating unnecessary urgency.

✔

Enable Two-Factor Authentication on important accounts.

✔

Never scan unknown QR codes requesting payments.

✔

Keep your browser and apps updated regularly.

✔

Verify customer support numbers from official websites.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about online scams, fraud prevention and reporting.

What should I do if I get scammed?

Stop communicating with the scammer, collect all available evidence, contact your bank immediately, and report the incident through ScamRadar and the official cyber crime portal.

Can I recover money lost to a scam?

Recovery depends on how quickly you act. Contact your bank as soon as possible and file an official cyber crime complaint without delay.

How can I identify a phishing website?

Check the domain name, HTTPS certificate, spelling mistakes, suspicious popups, fake login pages and unusually attractive offers.

Is HTTPS enough to trust a website?

No. HTTPS only encrypts the connection. Scam websites can also use HTTPS certificates, so always verify the domain and reputation.

Does ScamRadar verify websites automatically?

Yes. ScamRadar combines automated security checks, reputation analysis and community reports to help assess whether a website appears trustworthy.

Is ScamRadar free to use?

Yes. Anyone can verify suspicious websites, phone numbers, UPI IDs and learn about online scams without paying.
